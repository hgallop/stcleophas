Partners & Collaborators

Grateful for those who walk alongside us in ministry and hospitality

A Ministry Built on Collaboration

Saint Cleophas Outreach Ministry actively collaborates with local churches, community organisations, and civic partners in Martinsville and the surrounding region. These partnerships strengthen our ability to serve the community while fostering unity among people of faith who share a common commitment to compassion and service.

We are deeply grateful to the congregations who have opened their chapels to us, making it possible for us to gather for worship, pastoral care, and formation across Henry County and beyond.

Hosting Partners

These congregations generously provide us with space for worship and ministry. We are profoundly thankful for their hospitality and their spirit of ecumenical welcome.

Collinsville — Sunday Worship

Smith Memorial United Methodist Church

Smith Memorial UMC graciously hosts our Sunday Mass each week, providing their chapel as a place of worship for our community. Their generosity makes it possible for us to gather in Collinsville for the Sunday Eucharist.

2703 Daniels Creek Rd, Collinsville, VA 24078
Sunday Mass 3:00 PM

Martinsville — Weekday Worship & Office

First Baptist Church Martinsville

First Baptist Church Martinsville hosts our weekday worship, pastoral office, and formation programmes. Their chapel and office space are at the heart of our weekday ministry — from Mass and Morning Prayer to Bible Study and pastoral care.

23 Starling Ave, Martinsville, VA 24112
Wednesday Mass 12:00 PM · Office open Mon–Thu

Our Ecumenical Spirit

Saint Cleophas Outreach Ministry is rooted in the Anglican tradition, but our mission has always extended beyond denominational lines. We believe that the unity of the Body of Christ is made visible when Christians from different traditions work together in service, prayer, and witness.

Our partnerships with congregations of other traditions — Methodist, Baptist, and others — reflect our conviction that the Emmaus story belongs to the whole Church: walking together, sharing the Word, and recognising Christ in one another.

We welcome collaboration with any church, organisation, or individual who shares our commitment to the Gospel and to the dignity of every person.

“I appeal to you, brothers and sisters, in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ, that all of you agree with one another in what you say and that there be no divisions among you, but that you be perfectly united in mind and thought.”
1 Corinthians 1:10
